How does photosynthesis work?

Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert light energy into chemical energy. Here's how it works:

  1. Plants capture light using chlorophyll in their chloroplasts
  2. They use this energy to convert CO₂ and water into glucose and oxygen
  3. The chemical equation is: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

This process is essential for plant growth and produces oxygen that many organisms, including humans, need to survive.

What's the difference between C3 and C4 photosynthesis?

C3 and C4 photosynthesis differ in how plants fix carbon dioxide:

C3 Photosynthesis:

  • Most common form (85% of plants)
  • First product is a 3-carbon molecule (3-PGA)
  • Examples: rice, wheat, soybeans
  • Less efficient in hot, dry environments

C4 Photosynthesis:

  • First product is a 4-carbon molecule (oxaloacetate)
  • Has spatial separation of carbon fixation
  • Examples: corn, sugarcane, sorghum
  • More efficient in hot, dry environments

C4 plants evolved adaptations to reduce photorespiration, making them more efficient in tropical climates.
